## The Problem

Menswear moves fast but the signal is scattered. Trend reports are paywalled, social data is noisy, and by the time a trend hits mainstream retail coverage, it's too late to act on it. There's no single dashboard that tracks what's actually moving across brands and markets in real time.

## The Approach

Built an intelligence dashboard that aggregates signals from multiple sources: social mention velocity, search trend data, retail availability shifts, and editorial coverage. The dashboard ranks brands and categories by momentum, not just volume.

Key decisions:
- Focused on rate-of-change metrics rather than absolute numbers
- Built domain-specific sentiment filters to separate hype from genuine demand shifts
- Designed the UI around daily check-ins: what moved today, what's trending this week

## The Outcome

Live dashboard at menswear.michaelpyon.com. Tracks 50+ brands across streetwear, tailoring, and contemporary categories. The momentum scoring surfaces emerging trends 2-3 weeks before they show up in mainstream coverage.

## Reflection

The hardest part was defining "signal" vs. "noise" in fashion data. A brand trending on TikTok doesn't always mean sales are moving. The momentum algorithm went through 4 iterations before the rankings felt trustworthy. Next step would be adding price intelligence to correlate trend signals with actual market movement.
